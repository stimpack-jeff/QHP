# InformedDNA: AI Transformation Strategy

## Executive Summary

**InformedDNA** is QHP's tech-enabled genetic testing advisory and benefits management company, providing genetic counseling, utilization management, and precision health services to payers, health systems, and life sciences companies. This document outlines a comprehensive AI transformation strategy that could deliver **50-60% productivity improvement** in genetic counseling and utilization management while positioning InformedDNA as the AI-native leader in precision health services.

### Market Position & Competitive Landscape

**Market:** Genetic testing management and genetic counseling services - estimated $2-3B market, growing 15-20% annually driven by:
- Explosion in genetic testing volume
- Complex payer coverage landscape
- Shortage of genetic counselors (5,000 certified vs. 10,000+ needed)
- Precision medicine adoption
- Whole genome sequencing becoming more common

| Competitor | Focus | Competitive Position vs. InformedDNA |
|------------|-------|--------------------------------------|
| **eviCore (Evernorth/Cigna)** | Utilization management (broad) | Larger scale; InformedDNA more specialized in genetics |
| **AIM Specialty Health (Anthem)** | Imaging & specialty UM | Broader services; InformedDNA deeper genetics expertise |
| **Concert Genetics** | Genetic testing UM | Direct competitor; InformedDNA has counseling advantage |
| **GeneDx (acquired by OPKO/Sema4)** | Lab + genetic counseling | Lab conflict; InformedDNA independent |
| **PWNHealth** | Telehealth genetic counseling | Direct competitor; InformedDNA has payer services advantage |
| **Genome Medical** | Telehealth genetic counseling | Direct competitor; InformedDNA has broader services |
| **Avalon Healthcare** | Specialty UM | Competitor in UM space |

**InformedDNA Competitive Advantages:**
1. **Largest Independent Genetic Counselor Network** - 50-100+ board-certified genetic counselors (estimated)
2. **Specialty-Driven Approach** - Clinical guidelines by medical specialty, not just by gene/test
3. **Integrated Offering** - Both counseling AND utilization management (rare combination)
4. **No Lab Conflicts** - Independent, not owned by testing lab
5. **Tech-Enabled** - Platform approach, not just manual services
6. **Excellent Patient Experience** - NPS of 72 is exceptional
7. **QHP Backing** - Capital for platform investment and M&A

**InformedDNA Competitive Weaknesses:**
1. **Scale vs. eviCore/AIM** - Smaller than major UM companies
2. **Lab Relationship Complexity** - Must maintain relationships with all labs (no captive volume)
3. **Payer Contracting** - Long sales cycles for new payer relationships
4. **Brand Recognition** - Less known than GeneDx, Invitae (labs), eviCore (UM giant)

**Requirements:**
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all AI vendors
- Data encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls and audit logs
- Data minimization (only use data necessary)
- Patient consent for AI use (where required)

- **Claude API (Anthropic)**: BAA in place, HIPAA compliant
- **Snowflake**: BAA, HIPAA compliant data warehouse
- **Internal AI Models**: Trained on de-identified data where possible
- **Data Governance**: Clear policies on AI data use

### Clinical Decision Support (CDS) Regulation

**FDA Oversight:**
- Most InformedDNA AI qualifies as CDS, not medical device
- FDA does not regulate CDS if:
  1. Healthcare provider makes final decision
  2. Provider can independently review basis for AI recommendation
  3. AI does not acquire/process medical images

**InformedDNA Position:**
- All AI recommendations reviewed by genetic counselors or UM nurses
- AI provides rationale/references for recommendations
- Human-in-loop for all clinical decisions

**FDA 21st Century Cures Act:**
- Explicitly exempts CDS from medical device regulation if above criteria met
- InformedDNA AI likely exempt

### State Genetic Testing Regulations

**Considerations:**
- Some states require genetic counseling before/after certain tests
- InformedDNA already provides counseling, so compliant
- AI does not change compliance posture

### Insurance/Payer Regulations

**Considerations:**
- Payers subject to state insurance regulations
- Must demonstrate AI does not discriminate
- Transparency requirements for coverage decisions

**InformedDNA Approach:**
- Bias testing on AI models
- Explainable AI (provide rationale for decisions)
- Regular audits of AI decision patterns
- Payer contracts specify AI use and oversight
