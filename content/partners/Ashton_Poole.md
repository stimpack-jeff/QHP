##### About Business Development Companies (BDCs)
BDCs were created by the U.S. Congress in 1980 as specialized, closed-end investment vehicles designed to fuel economic growth by investing in small- to medium-sized enterprises. Key characteristics:
- Must invest at least 70% of assets in U.S. firms with market values under $250 million
- Required to distribute over 90% of profits to shareholders (avoiding corporate income taxes)
- Publicly traded on major exchanges, providing retail investors access to private company investing
- Provide both capital and managerial assistance to portfolio companies

##### Triangle Capital's Investment Strategy
- **Focus**: Private debt and equity investments in **lower middle-market companies**
- **Target Companies**: Businesses with $10-75 million in annual revenue
- **Investment Types**: Senior and subordinated debt, equity co-investments
- **Industries**: Diversified across sectors including healthcare, business services, manufacturing, and consumer products
- **Structure**: Initially operated as an internally managed BDC (rarer and considered more shareholder-friendly than externally managed BDCs)
