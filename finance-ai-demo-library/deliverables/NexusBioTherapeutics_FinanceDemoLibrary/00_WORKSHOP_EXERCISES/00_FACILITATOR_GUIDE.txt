EXERCISE 1 — PROCESS THE AP BATCH (12 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INTELLIGENCE TYPES: Computational → Reasoning → stops at Judgment

FOLDER: Exercise_1_AP_Batch/ (8 files)

FILES:
  INV_GoodwinProcter_20240105.pdf  — Legal services (clean, no PO needed)
  INV_Eversource_20240203.pdf      — Utilities bill (clean, no PO needed)
  INV_FedEx_20240108.pdf           — Shipping (clean, no PO needed)
  INV_ThermoFisher_20240315a.pdf   — Lab supplies (DUPLICATE — Invoice A)
  INV_ThermoFisher_20240315b.pdf   — Lab supplies (DUPLICATE — Invoice B)
  PO_VWR_20240408.pdf              — Purchase order for VWR lab supplies
  INV_VWR_20240422.pdf             — VWR invoice (PRICE DISCREPANCY vs. PO)
  PKG_VWR_20240418.jpg             — Receiving report (QTY DISCREPANCY vs. invoice)

PROMPT FOR PARTICIPANTS:

  "You are the AP department at Nexus BioTherapeutics, a clinical-stage
   pharmaceutical company. I'm uploading a batch of AP documents: vendor
   invoices, purchase orders, and receiving reports. Process the entire
   batch: (1) extract all key fields from every document into a single
   table, (2) match each invoice to its corresponding PO and receiving
   report where possible, (3) for each matched set, verify quantity and
   unit price agreement across all three documents, (4) assign GL account
   codes based on the vendor and line item descriptions, (5) generate the
   proposed AP journal entries ready for posting, (6) produce an exception
   report listing ONLY the items that require human review — with the
   specific reason each was flagged. Present a clean summary showing:
   X invoices processed, Y matched clean and ready to post, Z flagged
   for human review."

WHAT AI SHOULD PRODUCE:
  1. A data extraction table for all 8 documents
  2. Match results:
     - Goodwin Procter, Eversource, FedEx: standalone service invoices,
       no PO match needed → code and approve
     - Thermo Fisher A + B: DUPLICATE FLAG (same vendor, date, PO, items)
     - VWR PO + Invoice + Receiving: 3-WAY MATCH with TWO discrepancies
  3. Proposed journal entries for the 3 clean invoices
  4. Exception report with 2 flags

PLANTED ANOMALIES:
  Anomaly 1 — Duplicate Invoice (Computational):
    Both Thermo Fisher invoices = $5,506.50, same date, same PO-20847,
    same 4 line items, different invoice numbers (INV-101825 vs INV-101828).
    Duplicate exposure: $5,506.50.

  Anomaly 2 — Three-Way Match (Reasoning):
    PO: 4 cases × $47.50 = $190.00
    Invoice: 4 cases × $53.25 = $213.00  (price +12.1%)
    Receiving: 3 cases shipped           (qty short by 1)
    Correct payable: 3 × $47.50 = $142.50
    Invoice claims: $213.00
    Discrepancy: $70.50 (33% overbilling)

FACILITATOR TALKING POINTS:
  "How long would this batch take your AP team manually?"
  "Notice: AI didn't just find problems — it processed the clean invoices
   too. It generated journal entries. It coded to the GL. The human only
   reviews 2 exceptions out of 8 documents. That's the transformation."
  "What intelligence types did that require? Computational for extraction
   and matching. Reasoning for the three-way match — connecting information
   across three different documents to derive a conclusion. Where did AI
   stop? At the decision: approve, dispute, or escalate. That's Judgment."

EXERCISE 2 — BUILD THE CRO ACCRUAL (10 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INTELLIGENCE TYPES: Computational → Predictive → Reasoning → stops at Judgment

FOLDER: Exercise_2_CRO_Accrual/ (7 files)

FILES:
  Nexus_Company_Profile.pdf     — Company background and trial details
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_March.pdf   — Clean month (~$1.5M)
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_April.pdf   — Clean month
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_May.pdf     — Clean month
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_June.pdf    — Clean month
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_July.pdf    — Clean month
  CRO_NXTB301_2024_August.pdf  — ANOMALY MONTH ($2.25M — $400K+ over budget)

PROMPT FOR PARTICIPANTS:

  "You are the Controller at Nexus BioTherapeutics. I'm uploading six
   consecutive monthly CRO invoices for our Phase III clinical trial
   NXTB-301-001 (LUMINANCE-1), plus the company profile for context.
   The monthly CRO budget for this trial is $1,840,000. Process these
   invoices and produce the deliverables a Controller would normally
   prepare: (1) a budget-vs-actual table by cost category for each
   month, (2) a trend analysis showing month-over-month changes in
   each billing category, (3) the accrual journal entry you would
   recommend for September based on the pattern, (4) a variance memo
   explaining any month where total billing exceeded budget by more
   than 10%, and (5) an exception list of items requiring CFO review
   before the accrual is posted. Do not approve the accrual — that is
   the CFO's decision."

WHAT AI SHOULD PRODUCE:
  1. Six-month budget-vs-actual table (March through August)
  2. Trend showing stable months then August spike
  3. September accrual estimate (should AI use the spike or the trend?)
  4. Variance memo flagging August
  5. Exception list:
     - Pass-through costs at 145% of monthly budget ($933,800 vs $644,000)
     - Data management at 150% of budget ($414,000 vs $276,000)
     - Reference to "Protocol Amendment 3.1" with no supporting documentation

PLANTED ANOMALY (August):
  Monthly budget: $1,840,000
  August invoice total: $2,253,080
  Pass-through site costs: $933,800 (budget: $644,000) — +$289,800 / +45%
  Data management: $414,000 (budget: $276,000) — +$138,000 / +50%
  Invoice mentions "Protocol Amendment 3.1" — no amendment on file

FACILITATOR TALKING POINTS:
  "That budget-vs-actual table and September accrual — how many days does
   your Controller spend producing that deliverable each month?"
  "AI used Computational intelligence for extraction, Predictive for the
   accrual estimate, and Reasoning for the variance analysis. Where did it
   stop? At: should we approve the August invoice? Should we approve the
   September accrual given the anomaly? Those are Judgment calls — choosing
   between defined options with real consequences."
  "The deeper question — 'Should we even be running this trial?' — is
   Wisdom. AI will never ask that question."

EXERCISE 3 — DRAW THE LINE (8 minutes)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FOLDER: Exercise_3_Draw_The_Line/

No files uploaded to AI. Participants work individually (3 min) then discuss.
Use the printed handout or project the list on screen.

See Draw_The_Line_Handout.txt for the full exercise with answer key.

KEY DISCUSSION MOMENTS:
  - Items 1-3 are consistently rated "Full AI" — most people get these right
  - Item 5 (variance commentary) surprises people — AI drafts it well
  - Item 6 vs. Item 8: both are "human" territory, but for different reasons.
    #6 is Judgment (choosing from defined options about the trial).
    #8 is Wisdom (questioning the entire CRO frame).
  - Item 9 vs. Item 10: AI catches the violation (Full AI). But enforcement
    is a human call (Judgment → Wisdom).

CLOSING LINE:
  "Everything you marked 'Full AI' or 'AI + Human' — that's 60-70% of
   what your finance team does today. The question isn't whether AI can do
   it. You just watched it happen. The question is: what do you want your
   team doing instead?"

BONUS EXERCISE — EXPENSE REPORT (if time permits, ~5 min)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FOLDER: Exercise_BONUS_Expense_Review/

FILE: EXPRPT_Vega_NYC_Oct2024.pdf

PROMPT:
  "You are reviewing expense reports for Nexus BioTherapeutics. I'm
   uploading an expense report submitted by a sales employee for a New
   York client visit. Nexus policy states: (a) only business-purpose
   expenses are reimbursable, (b) personal care items and prescriptions
   are never reimbursable, (c) expenses must be incurred during the
   approved travel period. Review every line item and: (1) flag any line
   that may violate policy, stating which policy it violates and why,
   (2) identify any supporting signals that strengthen or weaken the flag,
   (3) calculate the reimbursable total if the flagged items are excluded,
   and (4) list the options available to the approving manager — but do
   not make the approval decision. Present as a structured memo."

PLANTED ANOMALY:
  Line 5: CVS Pharmacy — $127.40 — "prescription refill + personal care items"
  Submitted on Saturday October 5 — one day AFTER the trip ended (Oct 3-4)
  Three signals: (1) personal description, (2) weekend post-trip date,
  (3) non-business vendor

TEACHING POINT:
  Reasoning catches it. Judgment decides what to do. But ask:
  "Is your expense policy written clearly enough that this couldn't happen?"
  That's a Wisdom question.

GENERAL GUIDANCE FOR FACILITATOR
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

AI MODEL DIFFERENCES:
  Different AI systems may produce different results. This is intentional.
  Use it as a teaching moment about AI reliability:
  - Some models may miss a line item or misread a total
  - Some may hallucinate a discrepancy that doesn't exist
  - All should catch the major anomalies
  - The variance between models is itself a reason for human oversight
